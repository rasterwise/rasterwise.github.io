Use Case

Website Monitoring with Screenshots

Automatically capture screenshots at regular intervals to monitor visual changes, detect regressions, and ensure your websites display correctly across updates.

Get Started - From $5/mo Try Live Demo

The Challenge

Websites change constantly - deployments, CMS updates, third-party widgets. Without visual monitoring, broken layouts, missing images, and display issues go unnoticed until customers report them.

  • Visual regressions slip through testing
  • Third-party changes break layouts
  • No historical record of changes

The Solution

GetScreenshot captures your pages on a schedule, giving you a visual history. Compare screenshots over time, get notified of changes, and catch issues before your customers do.

  • Scheduled screenshot capture via automation
  • Full page capture for complete visibility
  • Webhook delivery for real-time processing

What You Can Monitor

Common website monitoring scenarios

Visual Regression Testing

Capture screenshots before and after deployments to catch CSS breaks, layout shifts, and missing elements.

Uptime Monitoring

Verify pages render correctly, not just that they return 200. Catch soft failures that uptime tools miss.

Content Verification

Ensure CMS changes, pricing updates, and promotional content display correctly across pages.

Third-Party Widget Tracking

Monitor how embedded widgets, ads, and external content affect your page appearance.

Cross-Browser Consistency

Capture at different viewport sizes to ensure responsive designs work across devices.

Brand Compliance

Monitor partner sites, reseller pages, and franchisee websites for brand guideline adherence.

Example: Daily Homepage Monitoring

Automated visual checks with Zapier

  1. 1

    Trigger: Schedule (Daily at 9am)

    Set up a daily schedule in Zapier to run your monitoring workflow at the same time each day.

  2. 2

    Action: GetScreenshot - Full Page Capture

    Capture your homepage with full page mode to get the entire page.

    url=https://yoursite.com
    fullpage=true
    width=1440
    hidechat=true
  3. 3

    Action: Save to Google Drive / Dropbox

    Store screenshots in a date-organized folder for historical comparison and audit trails.

  4. 4

    Action: Send to Slack / Email

    Notify your team with the daily screenshot for quick visual review.
