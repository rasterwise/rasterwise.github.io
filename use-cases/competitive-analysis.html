Use Case

Competitive Analysis Screenshots

Monitor competitor websites automatically. Track pricing changes, capture landing page variations, and document competitive positioning over time.

Get Started - From $5/mo Try Live Demo

The Challenge

Keeping tabs on competitors is critical but time-consuming. Manually checking websites, taking screenshots, and organizing them for analysis eats up valuable time that should go to strategy.

  • Manual competitor checks are tedious
  • Price changes go unnoticed
  • No historical record for trend analysis

The Solution

GetScreenshot automates competitor monitoring. Schedule captures of pricing pages, landing pages, and feature lists. Build a visual library of competitive intelligence over time.

  • Automated daily/weekly competitor captures
  • Full page capture for complete visibility
  • Organized archive for trend analysis

What to Monitor on Competitor Sites

Key pages and elements to track

Pricing Pages

Track price changes, plan modifications, feature additions, and promotional offers across competitors.

Landing Pages

Capture marketing messages, value propositions, and CTA variations to understand positioning strategies.

Feature Pages

Document feature launches, capability expansions, and product evolution over time.

Homepage Hero

Monitor messaging changes, headline tests, and brand positioning updates.

Customer Testimonials

Track social proof updates, case study additions, and customer logo changes.

Comparison Pages

Capture how competitors position against you and other market players.

Example: Weekly Competitor Pricing Monitor

Automated competitive intelligence with Zapier

  1. 1

    Trigger: Schedule (Weekly on Monday)

    Set up a weekly schedule to capture competitor pricing pages every Monday morning.

  2. 2

    Action: GetScreenshot - Capture Each Competitor

    Use multiple GetScreenshot actions to capture each competitor's pricing page.

    url=https://competitor1.com/pricing
    fullpage=true
    hidechat=true
    hidecookie=true
  3. 3

    Action: Save to Google Sheets

    Log screenshot URLs with timestamps in a spreadsheet for historical tracking.

  4. 4

    Action: Send Summary to Slack

    Share the weekly captures with your product and marketing team for review.
